Hoover Dam and Lake Mead

University of Kentucky Dep. Agricultural Economics

Hoover Dam was highest dam in the world when it was completed in 1935. It is located in Black Canyon, which is at the border of Arizona and Nevada. The dame created one of the largest artifical lakes in the world known as Lake Mead. As construction period coincided with the U.S. Great Depression, many unemployed workers had already moved to the dam's site along with their families seeking a job opportunity.

Specification of Hoover Dam

  • Original Name: Boulder Dam
  • Material: Concrete
  • Height: 221.3 meters
  • Reservoir: Lake Mead
  • Surface Area: 162,700 acres

Main Function of Dams

  • Agriculture: Irrigation for crops
  • Domestic Water Supply
  • Protect flood and Reserve water
  • Power generation plants: Hydroelectricity
  • Recreational Function (reservoirs): Aqua Sports, Fishing, Touring

Tools used in this project

  • PostGIS was used to store/query the national dataset.
  • Data is shown on Mapbox web map with geolocation services enabled. If the map got popular, it would require a fee to use.
  • Dam shape file is directly integrated into Mapbox.
  • Water Resource Data from USGS Water Mission Area NSDI Node
